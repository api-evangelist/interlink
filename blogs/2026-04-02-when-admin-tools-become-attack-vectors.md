What the Stryker Breach Reveals About Endpoint Management Risk A recent cyberattack against medical technology company Stryker highlights a growing risk facing organizations that rely heavily on endpoint management platforms. The incident affected Stryker’s Microsoft environment and involved the misuse of legitimate administrative tooling—an approach increasingly favored by threat actors because it blends in with normal operations. Endpoint management platforms play a central role in modern IT operations. They control how devices are configured, how software is deployed, and how administrative actions are executed at scale. When access to these platforms is not tightly governed, attackers may gain broad reach without deploying traditional malware.
